BUILT BY EXPLORERS, FOR EXPLORERS

It was never meant to become an app.

XPLOR began with six mates spending their weekends finding caves, climbing summits and wild camping around the UK. Then the challenge became bigger: complete every proper mountain summit we could find.

“We just needed one place to tick everything off and see how our lists compared.”

At first, we did what everyone does. We made lists in our phone notes. But everyone had different names, different photos and different ideas of what counted. There was no clear way to see who had completed what, where we had wild camped, which caves we had explored or what the group still had left to do.

We assumed an app already existed. We searched for hours and found hiking navigation, route planning and weather apps, but nothing that solved the simple problem we actually had: put all the locations in one place, tick them off, see them visually on a map and compare progress with friends.

So XPLOR started as a basic private list for the six of us. Then we kept adding to it. More locations. Wild camps. Caves. Progress. Profiles. Friends. Soon, other people saw it and asked for access. Then their friends wanted in too.

What began as a small tool for our weekends became the outdoor platform we could not find anywhere else.
